Monthly Workflow — Step by Step

Your monthly rhythm is simple: pull a new list from Property Radar, upload it to the portal, and let the system do the heavy lifting overnight. Here's exactly how it flows.

Part A — Uploading a New List

  1. Export your leads from Property Radar. Filter your search criteria as usual and export the results as a CSV file. Save it somewhere easy to find — your Desktop or Downloads folder is fine. Property Radar's export button is typically in the top-right of your results view. Choose CSV/spreadsheet format when prompted.

  2. Log into your portal at aiphorism-client-portal.vercel.app/jr-propstream.

  3. Click "Property CSV Import" in the navigation menu.

  4. Give your list a name. Use something descriptive and date-stamped — for example: "LA County April 2026" or "San Fernando Valley Q2." This name will appear as a filter in your Lead Pipeline dashboard, so you can always tell lists apart. Each upload creates a separate named list. You can have many lists active at the same time.

  5. Click "Upload" and select your CSV file. The portal will process it immediately — you'll see a results table appear showing how many properties were imported.

  6. You're done for now. The portal automatically queues every property for RPR validation, which runs overnight.

What Happens Next (Automatic) Overnight, the system runs each property address against RPR (Realtors Property Resource) to verify owner information is current. By the next morning, every property in your list will have a status badge: ✓ Match, ⚠ Changed, or ✗ Not Found. You don't need to do anything — just check back the next morning.

Part B — Reviewing Your Leads (Next Morning)

  1. Log into the portal and go to Lead Pipeline. Your newly imported list is now fully validated.

  2. Filter by your new list name using the list filter at the top of the dashboard. This narrows the view to just the properties you just uploaded.

  3. Filter by PRP Status: ✓ Match to see clean, validated properties ready for mailing.

  4. Scan for any Active (MLS-listed) properties. If a property in your list has come on the market, you don't want to send it a postcard — that would be awkward. Filter by market status "All" to surface any that show as Active, then mark them accordingly (see Section 4 for how to do this in the drawer).

  5. Let the team know you're ready. Once you've reviewed and flagged any Active properties, notify Joshua so the Lob.com mailing can be triggered for that list's batch.

Part C — Ongoing: Following Up on Warm & Hot Leads

  1. Anytime a homeowner scans your QR code or fills out a form on quickshortsales.net, the system automatically upgrades their lead status to Warm or Hot. You don't need to monitor the landing page — it's all handled.

  2. Check the Lead Pipeline dashboard regularly (daily or a few times per week). Filter by Lead Status: Warm or Hot to see anyone who has responded.

  3. Open the property drawer (click on any row) and review the homeowner's contact info, valuation, and any form responses they submitted.

  4. Follow up directly — call or email the homeowner using the contact info shown. This is where your expertise as a broker takes over.

  5. Update the lead status in the drawer after your conversation — move it to Nurture, Dead, or keep it Hot depending on where things stand.

Using the Filters

The filter bar runs along the top of the dashboard. Use these filters in combination to drill down to exactly the properties you want to work:

Filter Options What It Does
List All / [Your named lists] Shows only properties from a specific upload batch. Each Property Radar export you've uploaded appears here by the name you gave it.
PRP Status All / ✓ Match / ⚠ Changed / ✗ Not Found / Pending Filters by RPR validation result. Use "✓ Match" to see mail-ready leads. Use "⚠ Changed" to manually review ownership shifts.

Pro Tip A powerful combination: Filter by your newest list + PRP Status "✓ Match" + Market Status "Off Market" + Lead Status "Unassigned." This shows you exactly which fresh, validated, off-market properties haven't been touched yet — your next mailing batch.

Click any row in the dashboard to open that property's detail drawer on the right side of the screen. The drawer is where all the action happens for an individual lead.

  • Contact notes field — auto-saves whatever you type; use this to log your calls and conversations

Understanding RPR Validation Results

RPR (Realtors Property Resource) is the NAR's national property database. The system cross-checks every property you upload against RPR to confirm the owner information is current and accurate before any postcard goes out. This protects you from wasted mail spend and ensures your outreach is landing with the right people.

Validation happens automatically overnight after each upload. Here's what each badge means and what you should do with it:

✓ Match — Confirmed & Ready

The owner information in your Property Radar export matches what RPR has on file. This property is validated and cleared for mailing. No action needed from you — it will be included in the next postcard send automatically (as long as it's off-market).

⚠ Changed — Review Required

RPR returned data that differs from your Property Radar export. This could mean the property recently sold, ownership transferred to an LLC or trust, or a name discrepancy exists. You should open the property drawer and review the RPR notes to understand what changed.

  • If the property recently sold → mark it Dead or Archive it
  • If ownership changed to a business entity → review whether it's still a viable short sale candidate
  • If it looks like a minor data discrepancy → you can manually approve it; let Joshua know
Note "Changed" properties are not automatically mailed. They sit in a holding state until reviewed. This is intentional — you don't want postcards going to a new owner who has no distress situation.

✗ Not Found — No Match in RPR

RPR could not find a matching record for this property. This can happen with very new properties, unusual address formats, or data gaps. These properties are excluded from mailing automatically. No action required unless you want to investigate manually.

⏳ Pending — Validation in Progress

The property was just uploaded and RPR hasn't processed it yet. This is normal if you're checking the portal the same day you uploaded. Check back the next morning — Pending will be gone.

Quick Rule of Thumb Focus your energy on ✓ Match properties. Skim the ⚠ Changed ones for any easy wins. Don't stress about ✗ Not Found — the system handled it.

When a Lead Goes Warm or Hot

This is where the system pays off. Every postcard you send drives homeowners to quickshortsales.net. When someone actually visits that page and takes an action — scans the QR code, fills out the form, requests more info — the system knows. And it tells you.

How Warm/Hot Status Gets Set

  • A homeowner scans the QR code on any of your postcards → tracked automatically
  • A homeowner visits quickshortsales.net and fills out the contact form → triggers an automatic status upgrade
  • High-intent form submissions (e.g., "I want to talk to someone") → set to Hot
  • Lower-intent actions (e.g., page view, partial form) → set to Warm
  • You can also manually set any lead to Warm or Hot from the drawer dropdown
You don't need to check the landing page yourself All engagement tracking is automatic. Your job is simply to check the Lead Pipeline dashboard for new Warm or Hot leads. The system surfaces them for you.

How to Work a Warm or Hot Lead

  1. Log into the portal and go to the Lead Pipeline dashboard.

  2. Filter by Lead Status: Warm or Hot. Prioritize Hot leads — these people have expressed intent and the window to reach them is short.

  3. Click the property row to open the detail drawer.

  4. Review their submitted information. The drawer will show what they entered on the form — their name, contact number, preferred contact method, and any notes they left about their situation.

  5. Make contact directly. Call or email them using the info provided. You're the broker now — lead with your expertise in short sales and distressed property situations. Speed matters here. Hot leads who don't hear back quickly will look elsewhere. Try to make contact within the same business day.

  6. Log your outreach in the notes field. Note the date, what was said, and next steps. This auto-saves.

  7. Update the lead status based on where things stand after your conversation.

A Word on Timing Homeowners in distress are often considering multiple options simultaneously. The window between "I scanned this QR code" and "I signed with someone else" can be shorter than you'd expect. Same-day follow-up on Hot leads is strongly recommended.

Postcard Campaigns

Every validated, off-market lead in your pipeline gets a postcard via Lob.com — a professional direct mail platform that handles printing, addressing, and USPS delivery. Here's how the campaign structure works and what you should know about timing.

The 3-Stage Campaign

Each property receives up to three postcard touches over a three-month period. The campaign is designed to build familiarity — most homeowners won't respond to the first mailer, but repeated touches dramatically increase response rates.

Stage 1
Month 1 — Introduction

First touch. Introduces Quick Short Sales and establishes that you're a trusted local resource for homeowners in difficult situations. Plants the seed.

Stage 2
Month 2 — Follow-Up

Reinforces the message. Homeowners who didn't respond to Stage 1 may be ready now — life circumstances change. A different angle or offer may be featured.

Stage 3
Month 3 — Final Touch

Last direct mail piece in the cycle. Creates urgency and a clear call to action. After this — if no response — the lead moves to Dormant status automatically.

What You Need to Know About Mailing

  • Postcards are only sent to properties with ✓ Match status and Off Market classification
  • Properties you manually mark as Active (listed on MLS) are automatically excluded from the mail queue
  • Mailing is currently triggered by the Aiphorism team — it is not self-service. Notify Joshua when a new list is ready to go
  • You can see the full mailer history for any property inside its drawer (which stage was sent, and when)
  • No response after Stage 3 → lead moves to Dormant. It stays in the system but no further mail is sent
Your Role in the Mail Process Your primary job in the mailing workflow is: upload the list, review it the next morning, flag any Active properties, and give Joshua the go-ahead. The rest is automated. You'll see postcard send dates recorded in each property's drawer once they go out.

Marking Properties as Active

If you notice a property in your list has recently come on the MLS — or you spot one while browsing — here's how to mark it:

  1. Click the property row to open the detail drawer.

  2. Find the "Market Status" field in the drawer.

  3. Change it from Off Market to Active.

  4. The property is immediately removed from the mail queue. No postcard will be sent.

Glossary

Quick definitions for the terms you'll encounter in the portal and in conversations with the team.

Short Sale
A real estate transaction where the property is sold for less than the outstanding mortgage balance, with the lender's approval. A short sale allows the homeowner to avoid foreclosure and settle their debt — though it impacts their credit. It's typically used when the homeowner is in financial distress and owes more than the home is worth.
RPR — Realtors Property Resource
A national property database maintained by the National Association of Realtors (NAR). It aggregates ownership records, tax data, valuation models, and listing history. The portal uses RPR to verify that the owner information on each property matches current public records before mailing — ensuring postcards go to the right people.
PRP Status — Property Record Parity
The result of the RPR validation check. Each property in your list is assigned one of four statuses: ✓ Match (records align), ⚠ Changed (something differs from your source data), ✗ Not Found (no record found in RPR), or Pending (validation hasn't run yet). Only ✓ Match properties are cleared for mailing.
Distress Score
A numerical indicator derived from Property Radar data reflecting how financially distressed a homeowner may be. Factors typically include mortgage delinquency signals, equity position, and public record indicators like NODs (Notice of Default). A higher distress score = higher likelihood the homeowner may be open to a short sale conversation. Use it as a prioritization tool, not an absolute filter.
CLTV — Combined Loan-to-Value Ratio
The total outstanding mortgage debt on a property divided by its estimated market value, expressed as a percentage. Example: $400,000 owed on a home worth $350,000 = 114% CLTV. Properties with CLTV above 100% are "underwater" — the homeowner owes more than the home is worth, making them prime short sale candidates. This figure appears in the property drawer.
Off Market
A property that is not currently listed for sale on the MLS (Multiple Listing Service). Off-market properties are the target of your campaign — homeowners who haven't yet listed but may be in distress. The dashboard defaults to showing Off Market properties because these are the only ones worth mailing.
Active (MLS Listed)
A property currently listed for sale on the MLS. Active listings are excluded from your postcard campaign — there's no point mailing a homeowner who's already working with an agent. If you find a property in your list that has gone Active, mark it in the portal drawer so it drops out of the mail queue.
Lob.com
The direct mail platform used to print, address, and send your postcards via USPS. Lob handles all fulfillment automatically — you never have to manage envelopes, stamps, or printing. The team uses Lob's API to trigger mailings for your validated lists. Send history from Lob is visible inside each property's detail drawer.
Property Radar
A real estate data and lead-generation platform that Jack uses to build targeted lists of distressed property owners. You can filter by geography, equity position, loan status, owner type, and more. The exported CSV from Property Radar is what you upload into the Quick Short Sales portal each month.
Dormant
A lead that has completed all three postcard stages with no response. The record stays in your system for reference, but no further outreach is triggered automatically. A dormant lead can be manually reactivated if circumstances change (e.g., new distress signals appear in a future Property Radar pull).
